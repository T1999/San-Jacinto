Hiking

Take on this 10 mile trail that is considered to be one of the toughest and most beautiful hikes in Southern California. The hike takes an average of 6 hours to complete and is a popular area for hiking so expect to see other hikers as well. Starting from long valley, there are numerous trails ranging from easy to very streneous hikes.

Winter Activities

Winters at Mount San Jacinto can vary depending on the season. Some winters produce several feet of snow while other seasons produce a dust or two. There are various snow-related activities that are available such as snowshoeing, sledding, skiing, and hiking. Make sure to contact the respective ranger station before performing any of these activities as well as filling out a wilderness day-use permit. It is also important to note that the ranger do not groom snowbound trails so be ready to navigate in your own with a map, compass, and GPS. Be sure to bring the necassary equipment as well such as microspikes, trekking poles, snowshoes, etc.

Camping

Mount San Jacinto has two developed campgrounds as well as 4 primitive wilderness campgrounds. Campers must have camping permits that are issued by Mount San Jacinto State Park.

Why Visit Mount San Jacinto?

Whether you are a hiker, camping enthusiast, or just want to expereince an awesome view, Mount San Jacinto offers something for everyone. Located right next to Palm Springs, Mount San Jacinto offers a variety of ways to get away from daily life and expereince nature.
